Your Airbnb Cleaner Just Cancelled.
Here's what to do right now.

Published June 2025 · Niagara Turnover Co. · 5 min read

It's 7am. Your guest checks in at 3pm. Your cleaner just texted that they can't make it. If you're a Niagara Airbnb host, this guide walks you through exactly what to do — in the right order.

Don't panic. Act in order.

The biggest mistake hosts make when a cleaner cancels is spending the first 30 minutes trying to fix the cleaner situation instead of finding actual coverage. Your cleaner is no longer your problem. Getting the unit turned over before 3pm is your problem.

Here's the order of operations:

  • Stop texting your cleaner — it's not going to un-cancel them
  • Check your guest's check-in time — this determines how much runway you have
  • Text a backup service immediately — every minute matters for same-day windows
  • Let your guest know you're "finalizing prep" — don't mention the issue, just set a check-in confirmation expectation

Why your usual options won't work

Facebook groups and neighbourhood apps

You'll spend 45 minutes posting and waiting for replies, then another 30 vetting whoever responds. By then you've burned half your window. People who respond to same-day Facebook posts are not the ones you want walking through your guests' bedroom.

Other cleaning services

Most cleaning companies book 48–72 hours in advance. The ones who will take a same-day booking are usually overbooking themselves and treating your job as the lowest priority. "We'll try to get someone there" is not coverage — it's a coin flip.

Doing it yourself

If you're local and you have the time, this works. But if you're managing a 3-bedroom unit with laundry, a full clean, and staging, you're looking at 3–4 hours of physical work. That's before accounting for travel, restocking, and photo documentation. Most hosts underestimate how long this takes until they're halfway through and realize their guest is 90 minutes away.

What "backup coverage" actually means

A backup coverage system is not the same as a replacement cleaner. It's a service that has crew on standby specifically for same-day and emergency dispatch. The difference:

  • A replacement cleaner has to be found, confirmed, briefed, and dispatched — all in real time
  • A backup coverage system has crew already allocated and can dispatch within hours
  • Backup coverage includes photo confirmation when complete — so you know the unit is actually ready before your guest arrives

What to say when you text for coverage: "I have an Airbnb in [city] — cleaner cancelled this morning. Guest check-in is at [time]. Here's the listing: [link]. Can you confirm coverage?" That's it. A real coverage service will tell you within 15 minutes whether they can take the job.

What to tell your guest

Don't explain the cleaner situation. Your guest doesn't need to know. A simple message is enough:

"Hi [name] — just confirming your check-in at [time]. We're doing final prep on the unit now. I'll send a check-in ready message when we're done. Looking forward to having you."

This sets a clear expectation without creating anxiety. If coverage is confirmed and running on time, you send the ready message. If there's a delay, you have information to work with.

After this — don't let it happen again

One cancellation is a problem. Two is a pattern. Most hosts who've been through a same-day cancellation scramble once change how they operate. The options are:

  • Keep a secondary cleaner relationship warm — someone you've paid before and can text directly
  • Onboard with a coverage service that has backup built in — so the backup dispatch happens automatically, not manually
  • Build a longer buffer into your calendar between checkout and check-in — not always possible, but it buys time

The hosts in Niagara who don't panic when a cleaner cancels are the ones who already have a backup relationship in place. They don't have to find coverage — they just notify it.
